2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies help websites recognize your device and store information about your preferences or past actions.

2.1 Types of Cookies

Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser

Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device until they expire or you delete them

First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you're visiting (speedflow.cc)

Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services (e.g., Google Analytics, Hotjar)

6. How to Control Cookies

6.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit speedflow.cc, you will see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences

Strictly Necessary Cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the Service to function.

6.2 Changing Your Preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by:

  • Clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the footer
  • Deleting cookies through your browser settings
  • Using our preference center (accessible from any page)

6.3 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each session
  • Receive notifications when cookies are set

How to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome:Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data

Mozilla Firefox:Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data

Safari:Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data

Microsoft Edge:Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

Note: Blocking cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of the Service.

6.4 Opt-Out Tools

Google Analytics Opt-Out:Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

Hotjar Opt-Out:Visit: https://www.hotjar.com/policies/do-not-track/

6.5 Do Not Track (DNT)

We respect Do Not Track (DNT) signals. If your browser sends a DNT signal, we will not load analytics or marketing cookies without your explicit consent.
